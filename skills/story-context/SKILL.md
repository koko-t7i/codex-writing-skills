# Story Context

Good writing tasks depend on the right context. Too little context causes contradictions; too much context buries the task.

## Context Sources

Prefer project-local files when available:

- `story/`: chapters, scenes, previous drafts
- `wiki/`: finalized reader-facing reference pages
- `kb/characters/`: character state, voice, relationships
- `kb/world/`: settings, factions, magic systems, technology, lore
- `kb/timeline/`: chronology and causality
- `kb/styles/`: prose style, dialogue, POV, genre conventions
- `kb/canon/`: hard facts and constraints
- `work/`: temporary outlines, brainstorms, critiques, revision notes

## Selection Rules

- Writers need the brief, recent prose, style guides, POV rules, and relevant canon.
- Critics need the draft, the requested focus, and reference material for that focus.
- Continuity checks need canon, timeline, character state, and any relevant earlier scenes.
- Wiki work needs confirmed sources and citations, not speculative notes.
- Brainstorming needs constraints, open questions, and what must not change.

## Materialize Important Decisions

If a choice will affect future chapters, put it in a durable file before relying on it later. Store decisions near the artifact they affect, usually in `kb/`, `wiki/`, or `work/`.

Record:

- what was decided;
- what was rejected;
- why it matters;
- which draft, scene, or canon area it affects;
- what remains open.
