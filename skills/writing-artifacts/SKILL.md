# Writing Artifacts

Use plain project folders so writing context survives across sessions.

## Durable Files

- `story/`: manuscript chapters, scenes, and durable drafts.
- `wiki/`: polished reader-facing reference pages for finalized canon.
- `kb/`: author-facing working knowledge.
- `kb/styles/`: prose style, dialogue style, POV, and voice guides.
- `kb/characters/`: character state, relationships, motivation, voice notes.
- `kb/world/`: worldbuilding, magic systems, locations, factions, culture.
- `kb/timeline/`: chronology, causality, dates, travel, sequence constraints.
- `kb/canon/`: hard facts that should constrain future writing.
- `kb/issues/`: recurring craft, continuity, pacing, or voice problems.

## Temporary Work

- `work/brainstorm/`: exploratory options and unresolved possibilities.
- `work/outline/`: arcs, chapter plans, beat sheets, scene briefs.
- `work/drafts/`: temporary drafts and revision passes.
- `work/critique-reports/`: critique notes and synthesis.
- `work/research/`: external research notes and source summaries.

## Rules

- Keep exploratory material out of `wiki/` until the user confirms it.
- Put decisions near the files they affect rather than in a disconnected master log.
- Promote recurring critique findings into `kb/issues/` when they matter beyond one passage.
- Preserve user files and existing canon; do not overwrite unless asked.
